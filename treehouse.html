treehouse cafe

Co-Creator: Atlas Zania

A collaborative 3D modeling and fabrication project designed to look like a whimsical forest.

My role in the group was the 3D modeler and designer for the top floor, assisting in planning and construction of the general structure as well.

treehouse cafe, or "folk tree" was a 3D model consisting of various fabrication methods, the main ones being 3D printing and laser-cutting. The construct is a whimsical forest cafe for little creatures that fit inside a locker after thorough measurement.

The second floor was finalized with an oven and a fridge with a salamander chef seated inside ready to send out any orders that needed to be fulfilled. There is a sink and other kitchen utensils that Mr. Solomon (the salamander) would be using in his kitchen, like spatulas, cups, and plates. There are also mushroom-shaped shelves lining the back wall ready to hold anything that might need to be stored. Another thing that was added later on was a sweet little balcony that guests could use to look out into the forest.

Each component was either hand-modeled or modeled on Fusion 360 and 3D printed. Regardless of their creation, each element was carefully measured and scaled to fit the size and weight requirements. Materials used were plywood, filament, craft pieces, paint, cardboard, sticks and greenery, lighting, hotglue, clay, and resin. Below are the CAD Drawings used to 3D print or model pieces.
